We are planning to introduce a Jiyu Waza
Challenge starting from next week (29/9) or the week after, and running
until the end of the year — about 11–12 weeks in total. 

The idea is to set aside 10–20 minutes
after regular Monday training for this challenge.


**Purpose:**

The goal is to sharpen our reactions, deepen familiarity with techniques, and,
most importantly **strengthen and refine the flow of Ki**.
The practice will be structured in a continuous, flowing way, with each Tori
receiving at least two rounds of practice.

Since this is our first time running such a
challenge, we may adjust the structure along the way if we find a better
approach.

If it goes well, we may add weapons
practice in future challenges.


**Outline:**


- **Weeks 1–3**: Four techniques, followed by one *kkyo* and a *ninin gake* (5–6 moves).
- **Weeks 4–8**: Five techniques, followed by two *kkyos*, and a *ninin gake* (5–6 moves).
- **Week 9 onward**: Six techniques, followed by three *kkyos*, and a *ninin gake* (5–6 moves).
- **Final week of the year**: Each Tori performs any techniques and any *kkyos*, finishing with a ninin gake — all within 1 minute.


&nbsp;

**Challenge rules:**


- The sequence of techniques must be followed (this sequence will be published in a later post).
- Uke can attack with any *waza* (uchi or dori), but not the same one consecutively.


We’ll need **at least 3 Toris** to run
this challenge. I’m in — so we need two more!

👉 If you’re interested, please add your name with **+1** (e.g. *Seymour+1*) by
replying to email or in our LINE group..

Thanks everyone — I’m excited to train
together and have some fun with this challenge!
